You have come to the Swabian Circle, which was geographically fragmented as it did not include the Habsburg territories of Swabian Austria, the member states of the Swiss Confederacy, or the Alsace region west of the Rhine, which belonged to the Upper Rhenish Circle. Despite this challenging physical structure, however, the Swabian Circle operated effectively.

The activities of the circle included financial support for its members in cases of emergency. Johann Jacob Moser reported in volume 32 of his Teutsches Staats-Recht:

Von dem Schwäbischen Crays kan ich dises melden: Als An. 1544 die Stadt Zell1 schier gar abbrannte, wurde ihre von gemeinen Schwäbischen Crayses wegen 300. fl zur Brand=Steuer gegeben.
Regarding the Swabian Circle, I can report the following: In 1544, when the city of Zell1 almost completely burned down, it was granted 300 gulden from the common funds of the Swabian Circle as a fire tax.

In addition, the Swabian Circle was busy with the military defence of its regions against the French expansion of the late 17th century. This implied that all estates immediately subject to the Empire had to pay taxes to support the troops. However, this was not always easy. Again, Johann Jacob Moser writes in volume 32:

Anno 1689. ist der Stadt Heilbronn, in Consideration der bey jetziger Französischen Irruption erlittenen Trangsalen einiger Nachlaß gegönnet, jedoch, daß sie dagegen mit denen lauffenden Crays=Praestandis fleißig beyhalten und die verfallene oder von denen Franzosen ruinirte Fortifications=Wercke nach äusserster Vermöglichkeit repariren sollte, erinnert und die verlangte Vorschrifft an Ihre Kayserliche Majestät erlassen wurden.
In the year 1689, the city of Heilbronn was granted some relief in consideration of the hardships suffered during the current French invasion, but on the condition that it diligently continue to pay the ongoing imperial circle taxes and repair the dilapidated or French-ruined fortifications to the best of its ability. This was communicated, and the required directive was issued to His Imperial Majesty.

After 1694, the imperial circles even maintained a joint army based at the Kehl fortress.

  1. Zell was the smallest imperial city in the Holy Roman Empire. ↩
