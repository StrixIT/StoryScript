Jost de Negker was a Flemish publisher and printmaker. He was born in around 1485 and died before October 1548. His birth place most likely is Antwerp. During his professional career, he specialised in woodcuts, which were common for maps and other printed images in the late 15th and early 16th centuries. Due to his reputation as a skilled craftsman, he was put in charge of Emperor Maximilian's block-cutting establishment in Augsburg in 1508/12.
