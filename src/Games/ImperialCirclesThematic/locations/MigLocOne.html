Migration & cross-border economies

Movement of goods and people was a constant feature of the early modern world although most people travelled on foot: merchants, soldiers, seasonal workers, refugees, and emigrants crossed vast territories. The Imperial Circles became crucial arenas for managing mobility in Central Europe as they defined who could pass, settle, or had to be expelled. Similar to our present day, early modern migration debates were linked labour loss, security, and demographic decline, especially after major wars. You will follow debates about emigration, unwanted immigration, and government recruitment of labourers abroad. You will read excerpts from imperial edicts, Circle mandates, and local responses that reveal conflicting interests. Can you balance economic needs with fears of depopulation and disorder, also drawing on your knowledge about modern migration policies?
