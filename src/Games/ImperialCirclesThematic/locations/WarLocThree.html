Louis XIV and France as the Empire’s Archenemy Louis XIV of France didn’t just want to be the most powerful king in Europe— he wanted to redraw the map. When he invaded the Palatinate, a German principality on the river Rhine, in 1688, the Empire had enough. The Imperial Diet declared war, and the Circles were ordered to raise armies. But unity was hard to find. Some princes, like the Electors of Bavaria and Cologne, openly sided with France. Even when the Diet approved a massive troop quota, internal and financial disputes weakened the Empire’s response.
