Wars against the Ottoman Empire - a Never-Ending Struggle? While France became the archenemy of many German-speaking princes in the later 17th century, the Ottoman Empire was the Holy Roman Empire’s most feared enemy between 1529 and 1699. The two powers clashed repeatedly, and the Habsburg lands were especially exposed to Ottoman expansion. The Imperial Circles were supposed to rally troops and funds to defend Christendom, but the reality was complex.
