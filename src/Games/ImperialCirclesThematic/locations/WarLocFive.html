The 1757 War Against Prussia – an Empire Divided? Frederick the Great of Prussia was a thorn in the Empire’s side. When he invaded Saxony in 1756, the Imperial Diet declared war— this time, against one of its own members. The Circles were supposed to enforce the peace, but Prussia’s army was too strong, and the Reichsarmee was too slow. The war became part of the larger Seven Years’ War, fought in Europe as well as overseas European colonies. It is often considered the real first world war by early modern historians. By the time it ended, the Empire’s authority had taken a serious blow, and the rise of unified nation states across Europe began.
