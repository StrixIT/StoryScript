Currencies & monetary fraud

Money was one of the most visible and destabilising problems of early modern governance. Debased coins, illegal minting, and cross-border circulation of fraudulent currencies threatened trust, trade, and social peace. The Imperial Circles played a central role in inspecting coins, issuing mandates, and organising so-called “Münzprobationstage” (convention to approve currencies). Visiting several places on our map, you will investigate how authorities identified “bad money,” coordinated coin bans across territories, and tried to enforce compliance among merchants and subjects. While early modern monetary governance is in many ways a success story of transregional regulation, you also have to be prepared to see the limits of control and the devastating consequences of currency crises for the people.
