Poverty & Social Order

In the early modern period, poverty was not only a social issue but a problem of security and public health, as poor people travelling from place to place were linked with crime and the spread of contagious illness. The Imperial Circles, therefore, coordinated policies against vagrancy, begging, and so-called “masterless people.” At the same time, authorities recognised obligations of care, especially in times of war and crisis. Regulation therefore oscillated between exclusion, punishment, and provision. During your journey, you will encounter ordinances targeting beggars, itinerant groups, and displaced soldiers, which were often issued jointly by several Circles. These ordinances gives you an insight into early modern fear, morality and discipline, but also causes of poverty. Can you navigate these sources and help the Empire balance demands for public order and support for those in need?
