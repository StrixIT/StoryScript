The French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars - the Empire’s Last Stand By the 1790s, the French Revolution had turned Europe upside down. Monarchs in neighbouring countries were worried about the increasingly radical tone of Republican voices and the execution of the French royal family. The Imperial Diet declared war in 1793 and again in 1799, but many princes refused to fight. The Reichsarmee was no match for France’s revolutionary armies. In 1806, after years of defeat, the Holy Roman Empire was dissolved as a formal political union, which also brought an end to the Imperial Circles and their role in collective defence.
