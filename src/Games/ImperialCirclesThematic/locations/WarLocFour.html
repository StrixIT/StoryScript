The War of the Polish Succession – from Family Feud to European War When the Polish throne became vacant in 1733, Europe’s powers picked sides in the conflict between two potential successors. The Empire backed Augustus III of Saxony, one of their own powerful princes, while France supported Stanisław Leszczyński. The Diet approved extra troops, which should ideally have resulted in a collectively managed Reichsarmee (Imperial Army). However, many princes sent their armies directly to Austria instead, showing their support for the Habsburg Emperor as their leader, but also undermining the more federal principles of the imperial union. The Imperial Circles as administrative units were essentially bypassed.
